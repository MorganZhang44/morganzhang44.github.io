Doing research effectively is as much about **systems** as it is about ideas. Over the years, I've iterated on my workflow until it became something I genuinely enjoy. Here's what it looks like today.

## Literature Management

Finding, reading, and citing papers is a huge part of the job.

- **Zotero** — My reference manager of choice. The browser connector makes saving papers effortless, and it syncs across devices.
- **Semantic Scholar** — For discovering relevant papers and tracking citations.
- **Notion** — I keep a "Paper Notes" database where I summarize every paper I read with key takeaways and relevance to my work.

## Writing

- **LaTeX + Overleaf** — All my papers, theses, and reports are written in LaTeX. Overleaf makes collaboration seamless.
- **Grammarly** — A quick pass for grammar and clarity, especially for camera-ready submissions.
- **This blog (Hugo)** — For informal writing, tutorials, and thinking out loud.

## Experiment Tracking

Running and comparing experiments is critical when fine-tuning models or testing robot configurations.

- **Weights & Biases** — Logging metrics, hyperparameters, and system info. The comparison dashboards are invaluable.
- **Git** — Every experiment gets a branch. Reproducibility starts with version control.
- **Makefile / Shell scripts** — I script every experiment so that anyone can reproduce it with one command.

## Coding Environment

- **VS Code** — With extensions for Python, C++, Docker, LaTeX, and remote SSH.
- **tmux** — For managing multiple terminal sessions on remote servers.
- **Docker** — All non-trivial projects get a Dockerfile for reproducibility.

## Daily Habits

| Habit | Why |
|---|---|
| Morning literature scan (15 min) | Stay current without falling down rabbit holes |
| Daily log in Notion | Track what I did, what's blocked, next steps |
| Weekly review | Zoom out, re-prioritize, clean up loose ends |
| Time-boxing deep work | 90-minute blocks with breaks to maintain focus |

## Advice for Early Researchers

1. **Write more than you think you need to.** Notes, summaries, draft sections — writing is thinking.
2. **Automate repetitive tasks early.** If you've done it three times, script it.
3. **Don't optimize your setup endlessly.** Find tools that work and stick with them.
